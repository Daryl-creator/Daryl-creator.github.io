Foundations Reflection




What are three new things you have learned about yourself and your ego as a result of the core learning?



  • 1. I have to stop feeling bad about looking at others peoples code when learning, it is a way of learning and sometimes it is good to look at someone who has done it before for inspiration. As long as you can understand what the code is doing and you are not copying the code directly.
  • 2. The EGO tends to resurface when I am tired or hungry or have been stuck on a problem for too long and that the best thing to do at that point is to walk away and come back and try again another time or day.
  • 3. I have learned to be kinder to myself when I found the learning tough. One technique I found helps is to reflect on what I learn that day/week/month that I didn't know prior. It is easier on the soul when you reflect on how far you've come rather than always thinking about how much you have to learn.


What are the role of values, empathy and self awareness in learning and programming?



Your values determine how much you see the importance or worth in something. If you see learning and programming of high value, you will put in the time and effort to learn it no matter how tough it gets. A high level of empathy is needed in learning to program and working with others who are also learning to program. Learning such a demanding skill, the likelyhood is that you are going to be working with people who are under stress or pressure. A high level of empathy will help everyone learn cohesively. Self awareness goes in tandem with Empathy, your team members will respond to your emotions in different ways. Especially as a leader, a strong sense of self awareness will help you recognise your strengths and weaknesses and therefore gain the trust of your team members.
