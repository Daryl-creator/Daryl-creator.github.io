Problem Solving / S-DODAR




Tell us about a time you got stuck on a simple problem.



I was stuck on the superFizzBuzz kata for a while. I was new to Coding and didn't know how to approach a problem. I used the rubber ducky method and pseudocade but i felt that i was still missing the structure to approaching problem solving. I was feeling uncomfortable and out of my depth. I started reading researching problem solving and reliased I could try modifying a management model I was using in my old industry to see if I could use it to approach a coding problem and found that it worked! I learnt that I could use previous experience methods to hang my hat on whilst I was still building my experience in coding.


How did you solve a problem in an elegant way - S-DODAR





Using the SuperFizzBuzz problem as an example. I applied my model to working out the problem as shown below:

  • S - Self - Stretch, take a walk, eat something. Sometimes we're so in depth with problem solving that we don't even realise we have hit a limit and need to take a step back to reset and see this bigger picture.
  • D - Diagnose - I asked myself "What is the problem?", and i was very detailed and deliberate with asking this question, even down to looking at every character to check for syntax and case descrepencies. I wrote down my answers until I could logically see the problem and the steps to tackle it. I was Pseudocoding but also planning.
  • O - Options - This is where i look at the various options to tackling the problem. I had all the options listed in a later part of this article. In this case I decided that Google was the best step.
  • D - Decide - After I decided my next step I set about thinking about i was going to implement it. How I was going to phrase the question and what I expected to see.
  • A - Assign - This would be the step I would assign tasks to my team however it works just as well on your own. I listed the tasks that needed to be done i.e. find the answer, implement the code, check the syntax, look at it's control flow.
  • R - Review - Here the tests helped me alot, was it working? If not why? I linked this step back into the Diagnose part if it wasn't and keep looping through the steps until I solved the problem. If it was taking some time, i would go back to 'S' and go from there.
    • What is DODAR?

    DODAR is the process used in the Aviation industry by Pilots and Crew to assist in decision making during an abnormal situation or emergency.

Problem Solving Techniques and Processes





I used all the following techniques and processes during my challenges. This is how I have found using them so far:


  • Pseudocode - I use this allot however i was drawing them on my Ipad. I will use more of the '//' comment method on the code as this will help other developers see my thoughts and problems when working on problems in a team.
  • Trying something - I was doing this all the time and then reverse engineering if I came accross the solution.
  • Rubber Duck - Definately one of my favourite technqiues.
  • Reading error messages. Here is a good example os using the technology to your advantage. I looked at it as the computer telling me "I'm not happy with the code you wrote and here's why".
  • Console.log - I definately used this alot, however I am learning specifically where to use it to see specific parts of the code rather than just using it as a final test at the end.
  • Google - My best friend.
  • Asking Peers - This is my PRIMARY method at the moment, I enjoy collaborating with others thus am always working on problems with others. I find that most of the times we can get to solution quicker as well as all of us benefiting from the learning outcomes of each others.
  • Asking Coaches - I have had to do this a handful of times If i got really stuck but found that if I implemented the other techniques above, I was solving the problems before needing to ask the coaches for help
  • Reflections - This is an essential part of my learning. At the end of the day I always ask myself "what did I learn today?" and "What will i bring to tomorrow?".
